LARK'S TOP ALBUMS

Slanted And Enchanted - Pavement

Fave Songs

  • Summer Babe (Winter Version)
  • Zurich Is Stained
  • In The Mouth A Desert

    • My Favorite Lyrics

  • "I learned the truth The truth of the words Truth I made for you because It's just as good" - Trigger Cut / Wounded Kite

    • I got into this album in January. I've always adored Pavement but this is the album that made them become my favorite band. There's an edge to it i hadn't heard in their songs before, that intruiged me and kept me wanting to listen more.

    Bee Thousand - Guided By Voices

    Fave Songs

  • The Goldheart Mountaintop Queen Directory
  • Smothered In Hugs
  • Echos Myron

    • This album has been played by my dad billions of times, and i've always just been attached to one particular song. (Smothered In Hugs.) He would constantly sing it while playing his guitar, but back in June he ended up getting tickets to see them. Initially i wasn't intruiged but decided to come along anyway, and realized how much this album truly meant to me.

    Kiss My Super Bowl Ring - The Garden

    Fave Songs

  • Lurkin'
  • Lowrider Slug
  • Kiss My Super Bowl Ring

    • I originally found out about this band from seeing how the members, who are two twins, looked a lot like an actor i liked. Once i'd realized i liked a few of their songs, i decided to dabble further. Their early work was very experimental, and every song and album had felt like a hit or miss depending on what you'd like. But since then, they've found thier sound, which is sort of everything. Each album is a different thing, but as of late they've been mainly experimenting with metal and punk.

    Is This It - The Strokes

    Fave Songs

  • Soma
  • Barely Legal
  • Hard To Explain

    • I love this album so much, not only because my father would play it all throughout my childhood, but also because it was rediscovered and overplayed throughout the beginning of quarantine when my family and I escaped to my grandma's stranded home in North Carolina. Each song brings me a wave of nostalgia, but from so many different times.

    Odessey And Oracle - The Zombies

    Fave Songs

  • Beachwood Park
  • Hung Up On A Dream
  • Butcher's Tale (Western Front 1914)

    • This is also an album introduced to me in my early childhood that i came back to later, as i found each song so deeply dreamy or sultry and comforting. There's something so beautiful about the yearning throughout the album, the different story each song tells, and even the beautiful album cover that the artist mispelled the original album name on (Odyssey And Oracle) to where they just decided to spell it wrong on their actual album.
